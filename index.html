Chapter 1

What is Claude?

Claude is an AI assistant made by Anthropic. Think of it as a brilliant, endlessly patient colleague who never gets tired and can help with nearly any writing, thinking, or research task.

✍️

Writes for you

Drafts emails, case notes, summaries, and responses in seconds — in your voice and at the right tone.

🧠

Thinks with you

Helps you figure out how to handle a tricky customer, what to offer, or how to escalate an issue clearly.

🌍

Translates instantly

Suunto customers are global. Claude reads and replies in Finnish, German, Japanese, French, and many more.

🔒

No memory between chats

Each conversation starts fresh. Replace real customer names and order numbers with placeholders when needed.

Chapter 2

Getting Started in 4 Steps

Up and running in under five minutes — no downloads, no setup.

1

Open Claude

Go to claude.ai in any browser. Works on your phone, tablet, or computer.

2

Sign in or create a free account

Use your work email if Suunto provides a team plan, otherwise a free personal account works great to start.

3

Type your request in the chat box

This is called a prompt. Write what you need as if texting a knowledgeable colleague. No special commands needed.

4

Read it — then refine it

If the first answer isn't right, just say: "Make it shorter," "More formal please," or "Add the warranty policy." You're always in control.

Chapter 4

Ready-to-Use Prompt Templates

Copy any template, fill in the highlighted parts, paste into Claude, and hit send.

📧 Reply to a customer complaint
You are a professional and friendly Customer Success specialist at Suunto, a Finnish sports technology company known for GPS watches, dive computers, and outdoor equipment. A customer has sent the following message: "[Paste the customer's message here]" Product and case details: [e.g. Suunto 9 Peak Pro, purchased 3 months ago, GPS not locking] Write a warm, empathetic reply that: - Acknowledges their frustration sincerely - Explains the next step we will take - Gives a clear timeline or expectation - Closes in a positive, brand-appropriate way Keep it under 150 words. Professional but human.
After Claude replies, try: "Make it more empathetic" or "Add a line about our 2-year warranty."
🌐 Translate a customer message
Translate the following customer message into English. Then give me a 2–3 bullet summary of their main issue or question. Customer message: "[Paste the message here]"
Works automatically for Finnish, German, Japanese, French, Spanish, Chinese, and many more languages.
📋 Summarise a long email thread
Below is an email thread between a Suunto customer and our support team. Please give me: 1. A 2–3 sentence summary of the core issue 2. What has already been tried or offered 3. Current status and any outstanding action needed Email thread: "[Paste the full thread here]"
Run this before a customer call so you walk in fully prepared — even on complex, multi-week cases.
🚀 Write an escalation brief
I need to escalate a customer case to our engineering or operations team at Suunto. Here are my notes: Customer / Case ID: [Name or ID — anonymise if needed] Product: [e.g. Suunto Vertical, firmware 2.1.7] Issue: [Describe in your own words] Steps already taken: [e.g. factory reset, app reinstall, warranty swap offered] Turn these into a clear, structured escalation brief a technical team can act on. Include a suggested severity: Low / Medium / High / Critical.
Saves 20+ minutes every time you escalate. The team gets everything they need on the first read.
💬 De-escalate a frustrated customer
I'm a Customer Success specialist at Suunto dealing with a very upset customer. Here's the situation: "[Describe what happened — e.g. watch stopped working mid-race, returned twice, customer feels ignored]" Please help me: 1. Draft a sincere apology that doesn't sound scripted or corporate 2. Suggest 2–3 things I could offer to make this right (within normal CS authority) 3. Write one short "de-escalation line" I could use if they call instead of email
Remember: you decide what to actually offer. Claude is brainstorming — not making commitments on your behalf.
📝 Log clean CRM case notes
Please turn my rough notes into clean, structured case notes ready to paste into our CRM (Salesforce / Zendesk). Use short, factual sentences. My rough notes: "[Write anything here — messy bullet points, stream-of-consciousness, voice note transcription]" Format as: • Issue: • Actions taken: • Resolution: • Next steps (if any): • Follow-up due:
Perfect right after a call when you need to log quickly and move on to the next ticket.
🤝 Email a retail / B2B partner
I need to write a professional email to one of Suunto's retail partners or distributors. Situation: [e.g. Informing them of a stock delay on Suunto Race, updating them on a warranty batch issue, requesting their Q3 order forecast] Partner name / company: [First name or company name] Tone: professional and collaborative Keep it concise — under 120 words. We want to maintain a strong, trusting relationship.
Adjust the tone down for close partners, up for new or formal relationships.
📖 Explain a Suunto feature simply
A customer has asked me about the following Suunto feature. They're not technical, so please use simple, everyday language with no jargon. Feature: [e.g. Training Load, FusedTrack GPS, Body Battery, Dive Planner, route navigation, Sleep Tracking] Also give one real-world example of how this helps an athlete or outdoor enthusiast in practice. Keep it to 3–4 sentences total.
You can paste Claude's explanation straight into your reply — just read it through first.
Chapter 5

Tips for Better Results

Small habits that make a big difference in the quality of Claude's answers.

🎯

Give Claude a role

Start with "You are a Suunto Customer Success specialist…" — it shapes the whole tone.

📏

Set a word limit

Say "under 100 words" or "3 bullet points" so the output fits exactly where you need it.

🔁

Ask for a rewrite

"Try again but warmer" or "Less formal please" — Claude never minds being asked again.

🧩

Give more context

The product, the issue, what's been tried — the more Claude knows, the better the answer.

👁️

Always review first

Claude is fast but not infallible. You are the expert — read it over before sending.

🔒

Protect customer data

Replace real names, emails, and order numbers with [Customer] or [#ID] if needed.

Do's & Don'ts

Keep these in mind every time you use Claude at work.

✅ Do

  • Give the full context of the situation
  • Say who the audience is (angry customer, logistics team, manager)
  • Iterate — ask follow-ups to improve the answer
  • Use it to brainstorm multiple response options
  • Edit the output before sending or logging
  • Use it to save time on repetitive writing

❌ Don't

  • Send Claude's reply word-for-word without reading it
  • Paste passwords, payment info, or credentials
  • Treat Claude as official Suunto legal or product policy
  • Expect it to know real-time stock levels or live order status
  • Rely on it for information about internal Suunto systems
  • Share confidential business strategy without approval
