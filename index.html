Chapter 1

What is Claude?

Claude is an AI assistant made by Anthropic. Think of it as a brilliant, endlessly patient colleague who never gets tired, always has time for you, and can help with nearly any writing or thinking task.

✍️

Writes for you

Drafts emails, summaries, and responses in seconds — in your voice, at your level of formality.

🧠

Thinks with you

Helps you figure out how to handle a tricky customer, what to say, or how to escalate an issue.

🌍

Translates instantly

Suunto customers come from everywhere. Claude reads and replies in dozens of languages.

🔒

No memory between chats

Each new conversation starts fresh. Don't paste confidential customer data without approval from your company.

Chapter 2

Getting Started in 4 Steps

You can be up and running in under five minutes.

1

Open Claude

Go to claude.ai in any web browser. No app to download. Works on your phone, tablet, or computer.

2

Create a free account

Sign up with your email. The free plan is plenty to get started. If Suunto provides a paid plan, use that email instead.

3

Type your request in the chat box

This is called a prompt. Just write what you need, like you'd text a knowledgeable friend. No special commands needed.

4

Read the reply — then refine it

If the first answer isn't quite right, just say so: "Make it shorter", "Sound more friendly", or "Add information about the warranty." You're in charge.

Chapter 4

Ready-to-Use Prompt Templates

Copy any template, fill in the highlighted parts, paste into Claude, and hit send.

📧 Reply to a customer complaint
You are a professional and friendly Customer Success specialist at Suunto, a Finnish sports technology company known for GPS watches, dive computers, and outdoor gear. A customer has sent the following message: "[Paste the customer's message here]" Their order/case details: [e.g. Suunto 9 Peak Pro, purchased 3 months ago, warranty issue] Please write a warm, empathetic reply that: - Acknowledges their frustration - Explains what we will do to help - Gives a clear next step - Closes in a positive, brand-appropriate way Keep it concise — under 150 words.
Tip: After Claude replies, you can say "Make it more formal" or "Add a line about our 2-year warranty."
🌐 Translate a customer message
Translate the following customer message into English. Then, in 2–3 bullet points, summarise the main issue or question they have. Customer message: "[Paste the foreign-language message here]"
Works for Finnish, German, Japanese, French, Spanish, and many more languages automatically.
📋 Summarise a long email thread
Below is an email thread between a Suunto customer and our support team. Please give me: 1. A 2–3 sentence summary of the issue 2. What has already been tried or offered 3. What the current status / outstanding action is Email thread: "[Paste the full email thread here]"
Great to run before jumping on a call — so you walk in fully prepared.
🚀 Write an escalation brief
I need to escalate a customer case to our engineering/product team at Suunto. Here are my rough notes: Customer: [Name or case ID] Product: [e.g. Suunto Vertical, firmware 2.1.7] Issue: [Describe what's happening in your own words] Steps already taken: [e.g. factory reset, reinstalled app, replaced watch] Please turn these notes into a clear, structured escalation brief that a technical team can act on. Include a suggested severity level (Low / Medium / High).
This saves you 20+ minutes every time you need to escalate a complex case.
💬 Handle a frustrated customer
I'm a Customer Success specialist at Suunto and I have a very frustrated customer. Here's what happened: "[Describe the situation in your own words — e.g. their watch stopped working during a race, they had to return it twice, they feel ignored]" Please help me: 1. Draft a sincere apology that doesn't sound scripted 2. Suggest 2–3 things I could offer to make this right (within reasonable CS authority) 3. Give me a one-sentence "de-escalation line" I can use if they call instead of email
Remember: you decide what to actually offer. Claude is brainstorming ideas, not making promises.
📖 Explain a Suunto feature simply
A customer has asked me to explain the following Suunto feature. They are not very technical, so please use simple, everyday language with no jargon. Feature to explain: [e.g. Training Load, FusedTrack GPS, Body Battery, Dive Planner, route navigation] Also give me one real-world example of how this feature helps an athlete or outdoor enthusiast in practice.
You can then paste Claude's explanation directly into your reply (after reading it over yourself!).
🔄 Write a follow-up email
I recently resolved a support case for a Suunto customer. Please write a short, friendly follow-up email to check in on them. Situation: [e.g. We replaced their Suunto 5 Peak under warranty. It arrived 3 days ago.] Customer's name: [First name] Tone: warm but professional The email should: - Ask if everything is working well with the new device - Invite them to reach out if they need anything - Be under 80 words
A simple follow-up email dramatically increases customer satisfaction scores.
📝 Write case notes
Please turn my rough notes into clean, structured case notes for our CRM system. Use short, factual sentences. My rough notes: "[Write anything here — even messy bullet points or a stream of consciousness description of what happened in the call or email]" Format as: • Issue: • Actions taken: • Resolution: • Next steps (if any):
Great for right after a call when you need to log quickly and move on.
Chapter 5

Tips for Getting the Best Results

A few small habits make a huge difference in the quality of Claude's answers.

🎯

Give Claude a role

Start with "You are a Suunto Customer Success specialist..." — it shapes the whole response.

📏

Specify the length

Say "under 100 words" or "3 bullet points" so you get exactly what fits in your reply.

🔁

Ask for a rewrite

Not quite right? Say "Try again, but more empathetic" or "Less formal please." Claude never minds.

🧩

Give context

The more you tell Claude (product, issue, what's been tried), the better the answer.

👁️

Always review before sending

Claude is fast but not perfect. You are the expert — read it over and adjust anything that feels off.

🔒

Protect customer data

Replace real names, emails, and order numbers with placeholders like [Customer] and [#12345] if needed.



Do's and Don'ts

Keep these in mind every time you use Claude at work.

✅ Do

  • Give Claude the full context of the situation
  • Tell it who the audience is (angry customer, engineering team, manager)
  • Ask follow-up questions to refine the answer
  • Use it to brainstorm multiple options
  • Read and edit the output before using it
  • Use it to save time on repetitive writing tasks

❌ Don't

  • Send Claude's reply word-for-word without reading it
  • Paste passwords, payment info, or sensitive credentials
  • Treat Claude's answer as legal, medical, or official Suunto policy
  • Expect it to know real-time info (current stock, live orders)
  • Rely on it to know internal Suunto systems or your CRM
  • Share confidential company strategy without approval
